High-volume recruitment pipeline at a national employment agency

Talent Acquisition · High-volume recruitment · B2B employer partnerships

Context

Portugal's national public employment agency, operating across 53 centres nationwide and responsible for labour market management, professional training, and job placement at a national scale. I worked as a Talent Acquisition and HR Coordinator, running end-to-end recruitment, employer partnerships, and candidate onboarding inside a structured, high-volume environment.

Challenge

Public-sector recruitment at this scale is a volume problem and an accuracy problem at the same time. A large daily flow of candidates has to be matched to active employer vacancies quickly, without the matching becoming careless. At the same time, the vacancy side has to stay full, which means continuous outreach to private-sector employers, and a share of the candidate base belongs to priority groups with specific access requirements and employment barriers that a generic match would miss. The brief was to keep all three moving: candidate flow, vacancy pipeline, and priority-group placement.

Approach

I screened and matched an average of 25 to 30 candidate profiles per day to active employer vacancies using the government applicant tracking system, applying competency-based evaluation criteria rather than keyword matching. The competency view is slower per profile, but it is what makes a placement hold.

On the vacancy side, I ran B2B outreach to private-sector employers, drafting personalised recruitment proposals and collecting structured job requirement data, role scope, salary, and schedule, directly from employers. That structured intake meant the vacancies I was matching against were real and well defined, not vague postings.

I managed intake and registration for new candidate enrollees: validating documentation, building profiles in the applicant tracking system, and running structured orientation sessions that moved candidates from intake to active placement status within the same working day.

For priority candidate groups, youth employment programme enrollees, foreign nationals, and long-term unemployed individuals, I applied eligibility-based sourcing, matching each profile to vacancies aligned with their specific access requirements and employment barriers rather than to the nearest open role.

Outcomes

  • An average of 25 to 30 candidate profiles screened and matched per day, sustained across the engagement
  • An average of around 15 placements per week
  • An active vacancy pipeline maintained through continuous B2B outreach to private-sector employers
  • New candidate enrollees moved from intake to active placement status within the same working day
  • Priority-group placements handled with eligibility-based matching rather than nearest-vacancy matching

Skills demonstrated

Full-cycle recruitment, high-volume candidate screening, competency-based evaluation, applicant tracking systems, B2B employer outreach, recruitment proposals, structured job requirement intake, candidate onboarding, priority-group placement, vacancy pipeline management.
