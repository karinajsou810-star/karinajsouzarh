Internal communications diagnostic and employee enablement program

Internal Communications · Diagnostic methodology · Training design

Context

Mercearia Elite Ltda is a nine-person hospitality SMB in São Pedro do Estoril, Portugal. Owner-operated, serving both restaurant and pastry. I engaged as an independent HR consultant to diagnose internal communication gaps and design an employee enablement program to address them.

Challenge

The company had strong product and a loyal local customer base, but inconsistent operational execution. TripAdvisor reviews showed recurring complaints about long wait times and inconsistent service delivery. Internally, the team operated without formalized communication protocols, no training program for service standards, and no feedback loop between customer reviews and operational changes. External brand presence was limited to social media, with no website or coordinated visual identity. The actual problem was not product quality. It was internal coordination and operational consistency.

Approach

I applied my 5-system internal communications diagnostic to map the gaps. The framework covers five operational systems: operational (communicate to function), identification (be recognized to be known), belonging (communicate to share values), audit and control (communicate to organize feedback), and commercial (communicate to sell). I gathered diagnostic data through an owner interview, an anonymous internal survey with five of nine employees responding, TripAdvisor customer review analysis, and an online presence audit across Instagram, Facebook, and Google.

The diagnostic revealed that internal communication was WhatsApp-only with no formal protocols, customer feedback was not connected to operational change, and external brand presence was fragmented. I designed an external campaign called "Descobrimento Gastronômico no Restaurante Mercearia Elite," which included a promotional video, a branded slogan ("Sabores Eternos, Essência Portuguesa"), an outdoor design, two flyers (one for the event, one for corporate group dining), and an institutional website with menu, reservation system, and customer feedback form.

I created a four-module internal training program delivered through a shared Teams folder with video content for each module: introduction to communication best practices, customer service standards, order organization, and effective communication. I also built a monitoring proposal outlining metrics for tracking internal protocol adoption, customer feedback loop effectiveness, and brand consistency across channels.

Outcomes

  • The diagnostic identified six distinct internal communication gaps across the 5-system framework
  • A four-module training program operational for ongoing employee enablement and skill reinforcement
  • External brand consistency established through slogan, visual identity, and dedicated website
  • A customer feedback loop created through the website form and ongoing review monitoring
  • Website live as an operational deliverable, serving as customer-facing communication infrastructure

Skills demonstrated

Diagnostic framework application, internal communications audit, customer research analysis, training program design, brand strategy, feedback system design, employee training delivery.

Annotated decisions

A 5-system diagnostic instead of a single-system audit

When customer reviews complain about long waits, it is easy to assume the problem is kitchen speed or order management. But long waits are often a symptom of poor internal coordination (operational system), lack of role clarity (identification), or weak communication standards (belonging). A single-system audit would have missed those connections. The 5-system framework forces the question: where is coordination breaking down? What do staff not know about the company's values? What feedback loops are missing? The customer complaint points to the symptom. The diagnosis requires examining all five systems.

A four-module training program instead of a one-time workshop

A single workshop creates a spike in awareness and behavior change that decays within weeks. Staff turnover means new employees never see the workshop. Recurring video-based training accessible in a shared folder creates ongoing access to skill-building. It also lets you monitor which modules are watched most, which suggests where the actual capability gaps are. A program does different work than an event.

An anonymous survey for the internal employee voice

In a nine-person team, named feedback is rarely honest. If an employee says "our WhatsApp communication is chaos," it feels like criticism of leadership. An anonymous survey lets people name problems without social risk. The response rate was sufficient to identify patterns while protecting the respondents. Named feedback often reveals what people think they should say. Anonymous feedback often reveals what they actually experience.

Customer review analysis as an internal HR diagnostic input

TripAdvisor comments about long waits are often treated as marketing data. I used them as an internal HR diagnostic instead. If multiple reviews mention waits, that points to an operational coordination problem. If reviews mention rude service, that points to either training gaps or selection issues in hiring. Customer feedback becomes a window into internal system performance. The marketing utility is real, but the operational visibility it produces is the more useful function.
