What you get
  • Role design: I define the role before sourcing, what it actually needs to do, what is must-have versus nice-to-have, what success looks like at six months, whether it is an engineer, a first GTM hire, or a product lead
  • A sourcing strategy matched to seniority and profile: the right channel for the role, not every channel for every role
  • Active sourcing, screening against your real criteria, and a shortlist with written evaluation notes
  • Interview coordination end-to-end
  • A documented hiring process you keep: scorecards, stages, and criteria, so the next hire does not start from zero
Who this is for

You are a founder, an operator, or the person who owns hiring at a startup or scale-up, making hires that matter: your first engineers, a product lead, an early GTM team. The wrong hire is expensive, and a generic recruiter who has not understood the role will cost you. You do not want a CV firehose. You want someone who scopes the role properly, sources the right way for the seniority, and leaves you with a process you can run again. You want a talent partner, not a vendor.

How it works
  1. 1

  2. 2
    StrategyA sourcing approach matched to seniority and profile. The channel fits the role: ATS databases, LinkedIn, referral networks, or job boards, chosen on purpose.

  4. 4

Where this comes from

In a consulting engagement with an executive search firm, I documented sourcing strategy across ten professional categories and matched channel choice to role seniority and technical profile. I mapped the full process from client intake to placement. I have also recruited at volume: 25 to 30 profiles screened per day and ten placed per week as an individual contributor at a national employment agency, with no placement errors across the engagement. I build the strategy and I execute it.
