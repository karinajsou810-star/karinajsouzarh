What you get
  • A structured diagnostic across the five places people ops breaks as you scale: hiring, onboarding, retention, internal communication, and operational visibility
  • A written findings report: what is working, what is breaking, and where the root cause actually sits, not just the symptom you noticed
  • A prioritized fix list, split into fix now and fix later, scoped to your stage and your budget
  • A clear recommendation on the next step that fits: a hiring sprint, a build, a retainer, or nothing yet
  • An honest read. If your people ops are fine for your stage, I will tell you that and we stop here
Who this is for

You are a startup or scale-up that grew faster than your people systems. Onboarding, hiring, retention, or internal communication is starting to break, and you cannot tell which problem is the root cause and which is a symptom. You do not want to commit to a build or a retainer before you know what you actually need. You want a clear outside read on what to fix first, before you spend on the fix.

How it works
  1. 1
    IntakeYou tell me what looks broken and what stage you are at. I look at your current tools, processes, and informal practices before forming a view.
  2. 2
    DiagnosticI run a 5-system communication diagnostic and structured interviews to find where a visible problem actually starts. What a company does is often different from what it describes doing.
  3. 3
    FindingsA written report. What is working, what is breaking, and the root cause behind the symptom you came in with.
  4. 4
    Prioritized planA fix list split into now and later, with a recommendation on the next step, or a clear answer that you do not need one yet.
The method behind this

Before designing any fix, I run three diagnostic tools I have used across hospitality, electrical services, and private services: a 5-system internal communication diagnostic, a structured 15-question interview that surfaces the rules a team follows but cannot articulate, and a baseline measurement survey. In a four-month consulting engagement at an executive search firm, the same approach turned a model that lived only in the founder's head into a documented framework the firm could teach and sell.
