The credential

What the work actually was.

For a year I sat inside the system that decides which candidates get forwarded to employers — and which ones don't. Portugal's public employment infrastructure, 53 centers, tens of thousands of active candidates. I built the screening criteria and ran them daily: 25 to 30 profiles, 10 placements a week.

My role was to build the criteria behind the decisions — not just apply them. What question catches a gap in experience. What phrasing in a CV signals a candidate hasn't read the job description. What a confident-sounding profile is actually hiding.

I ran placement cases across three protected candidate categories — youth employment, foreign nationals, and long-term unemployed — with zero eligibility mismatches across a full contract cycle. That throughput doesn't come from instinct. It comes from a system. I know what a recruiter is looking for because I built the system they use.

The review

What a review with me looks like.

I read your CV the way a recruiter reads it on the third profile of the morning: fast, with criteria already loaded, looking for reasons to skip. The review identifies what breaks that scan and what survives it.

You get written feedback on what is working against you — section by section — and specific changes to make before you submit again. No generic advice. No filler. The feedback is based on what actually stops a profile at the screening stage, not on what looks polished.

This works for candidates applying to roles in Portugal or internationally, in English or Portuguese. If you are a foreign national navigating the Portuguese job market, I have direct experience screening that candidate category and know what employers in that pipeline are looking for.
