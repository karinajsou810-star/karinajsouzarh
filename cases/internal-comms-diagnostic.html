Internal communications diagnostic and employee enablement program

Internal Communications · Diagnostic methodology · Training design

Context

A nine-person hospitality SMB in Portugal, owner-operated, serving both restaurant and pastry. I engaged as an independent HR consultant to diagnose internal communication gaps and design an employee enablement program to address them.

Challenge

The company had strong product and a loyal local customer base, but inconsistent operational execution. Customer reviews showed recurring complaints about long wait times and inconsistent service delivery. Internally, the team operated without formalized communication protocols, no training program for service standards, and no feedback loop between customer reviews and operational changes. External brand presence was limited to social media, with no website or coordinated visual identity. The actual problem was not product quality. It was internal coordination and operational consistency.

Approach

I applied my 5-system internal communications diagnostic to map the gaps. The framework covers five operational systems: operational (coordinate to function), identification (be recognized to be known), belonging (communicate to share values), audit and control (organize feedback), and commercial (communicate to sell). I gathered diagnostic data through an owner interview, an anonymous internal survey with five of nine employees responding, customer review analysis, and an online presence audit. The diagnostic produced a structured map of where coordination was failing and why it was visible to customers before it was visible to management.

The internal system gaps were the most operationally urgent. I designed a four-module employee training program covering communication best practices, customer service standards, order organization, and effective team communication, delivered through a shared Teams folder with video content for each module. The format was not a one-time workshop. It was designed for ongoing access: new staff can complete it independently on their first week, and existing staff can return to individual modules as needed.

A parallel workstream addressed the identification and commercial gaps the diagnostic identified. This produced an external brand campaign, a branded website with reservation and customer feedback functionality, and visual identity materials. Those outputs gave the business a customer-facing presence consistent with its operational standards.

Outcomes

The diagnostic identified six distinct internal communication gaps across the five systems, giving ownership a structured picture of where coordination was failing before it reached customer experience. The four-module training program is operational for ongoing staff development, with each module accessible independently rather than requiring scheduled delivery. The external workstream produced a live website with a customer feedback form, creating a feedback loop between customer experience and operational management for the first time. The company's online presence became consistent with its product quality for the first time in its operation.

Skills demonstrated

Diagnostic framework application, internal communications audit, multi-source data collection, training program design, feedback system design, employee training delivery, external brand communications.
