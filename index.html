What I do, and who I do it for.

I run full-cycle recruitment, and I build the People Operations around it.

I have recruited at two scales. On a one-year fixed-term contract at Portugal's national employment agency, I screened 25 to 30 candidates a day and placed around 15 a week, with B2B outreach to private-sector employers keeping the vacancy pipeline full. That second context is where the rest of the work comes from. When you are the only HR person in a small company, there is no system to hand a new hire to. You build it. Methodology

Three diagnostic tools I use to figure out what is actually broken before I design a fix.

The first is a 5-system framework I run when communication keeps breaking down somewhere a customer can see it. It maps internal communication across five operational systems: how teams coordinate work, how the company is recognized internally and externally, how values get shared day to day, how feedback flows up and down, and how the company sells. When something looks like a customer service problem, examining all five usually reveals where the breakdown actually starts.

The second is a structured interview I use when an organization has a strong intuitive practice but no documented model. Owners and operators often cannot articulate the rules that drive their decisions. Probing questions surface those rules. Field observation confirms them. The result is a documented operational model that matches what the organization actually does, not what it describes doing.

The third is a quantitative survey instrument I deploy as a baseline measurement before designing interventions. It draws on diagnostic work conducted across organizations in hospitality, food distribution, and private services to surface where communication quality correlates with disengagement. It gives you a concrete baseline before you spend on a fix.

All three tools build on applied HR theory (recruitment, onboarding, performance management, compensation, career planning) and organizational communication research, which I studied formally during my Bachelor's at the Polytechnic Institute of Leiria. They share an operating principle: what an organization actually does is often different from what it describes doing, and specificity is what makes a framework operationally useful.
