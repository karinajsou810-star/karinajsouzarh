What the work actually was.

For a year I screened 25 to 30 candidate profiles a day, matched them to live employer vacancies, and ran placement cases across three candidate categories — youth employment, foreign nationals, and long-term unemployed. Ten placements a week. Zero eligibility mismatches across a full contract cycle.

My role was not just to apply the criteria. It was to build them. What question catches a gap in experience. What phrasing in a CV signals a candidate has not read the job description. What a confident-sounding profile is actually hiding.

That throughput does not come from instinct. It comes from a system. I know what a recruiter is looking for because I built the system they use.

The review

What a review with me looks like.

I read your CV the way a recruiter reads it on the third profile of the morning: fast, with criteria already loaded, looking for reasons to skip. The review identifies what breaks that scan and what survives it.

You get written feedback on what is working against you — section by section — and specific changes to make before you submit again. No generic advice. No filler. The feedback is based on what actually stops a profile at the screening stage, not on what looks polished.

This works for candidates applying anywhere — in English or Portuguese. Wherever the role is, the screening logic is the same.
