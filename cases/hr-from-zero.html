The first HR system for a growing company that ran on paper

Operations · HR foundations · Documentation

Industrial automation and electrical materials distribution São Paulo, Brazil Four-person team across HR, operations and client management Growth stage · reporting to senior leadership

Starting point

A company that had grown without ever building an HR function. Personnel records were incomplete and paper-only, filing had no structure, and client and supplier follow-ups happened from memory.

Context

Industrial automation and electrical materials distribution. SPARK Produtos Elétricos is a Brazilian distributor of industrial automation and electrical materials, in a growth stage, operating with a four-person team across HR, operations, and client management. I was the HR administrator, reporting directly to senior leadership, responsible for HR documentation, supplier coordination, and client and supplier communication.

Challenge

The company had scaled without ever building a people function. Personnel records were incomplete and existed only on paper. Filing followed no structure. Client and supplier follow-ups ran on memory rather than any record. As the company grew, leadership had no reliable archive to work from, and staff were not following the records that did exist.

Approach

I rebuilt the HR documentation from scratch. I audited the incomplete paper records, standardized the filing structure, and created onboarding and personnel documentation where none had existed. I built a correspondence and relationship tracking system for 25 active client and supplier accounts, with follow-up timelines and escalation protocols for proposals and service follow-ups. For procurement I designed replenishment workflows and delivery tracking across seven external vendors.

Why start with an audit, not a template

Dropping in a generic HR template would have documented a company that did not exist. The audit showed which records were actually missing and how the team actually worked, so the system I built matched the company rather than an idealized version of it. That is the difference between documentation people use and documentation people ignore.

Outcomes

  • A 45% improvement in HR data and documentation systems against the disorganized paper baseline
  • A paper-only archive converted into a structured system leadership could access and rely on for the first time
  • Unresolved client and supplier communications reduced to zero across the full engagement
  • Zero supply disruptions across the full operational period
  • Onboarding and personnel documentation created where none had existed before

Skills demonstrated

HR documentation · Records auditing · Process documentation · Vendor coordination · Correspondence and relationship tracking · Onboarding documentation
