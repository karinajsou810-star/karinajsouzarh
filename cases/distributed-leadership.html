People ops from zero

Operations · Systems design · Distributed leadership

Private services organization — healthcare, maintenance and specialized services sector Lisbon, Portugal · UK-based remote ownership Permanent team of 3 · Network of 7 external providers Ongoing engagement · 8 years

Starting point

No documentation. No HR function. No on-site management. Ownership permanently based in the UK with no visibility into ground operations.

Context

A private services company operating in the healthcare, maintenance and specialized services sector in Lisbon, with ownership permanently based in the UK. Three permanent staff on the ground. A network of seven external service providers. No HR function had ever existed. I was brought in as the sole HR and operations person, reporting directly to UK-based senior leadership who were never on-site.

Challenge

Without any formal systems, the UK-based ownership had no real-time view into what was happening on the ground. Workload distribution, overtime, vendor performance, and Portuguese statutory compliance were tracked informally — through messages and memory. Leadership decisions about staffing and vendor retention were made on incomplete information. Communication between the UK ownership and the Lisbon operation had no defined structure, which produced recurring coordination failures.

The operational environment made the stakes concrete: this is a sector where coverage gaps have direct consequences. Scheduling failures in healthcare and maintenance services are not recoverable the next morning. The systems had to hold under pressure, not just on paper.

What I built

I built every HR and operations system from scratch, with no prior documentation, no external HR support, and no one to check the work on-site.

The core deliverable was an integrated tracking system covering team scheduling, external provider coordination, and operational budget monitoring. It became the single source of truth for off-site decision-makers — replacing the informal WhatsApp-and-memory approach with real-time operational visibility for UK principals who were never in the building.

I designed a governance model for distributed leadership: monthly reporting cadences, budget reconciliation protocols, and escalation procedures, built from scratch to match how the organization actually operated rather than how it was supposed to. The UK principals no longer needed to travel to Lisbon for routine governance.

I built a three-document onboarding toolkit for incoming staff — shift schedules, role-specific operational profiles, and external contractor coordination documents — that delivered full operational readiness on day one for every new hire and rotating contractor across eight years.

For vendor management, I created a performance evaluation framework covering the seven external service providers. Engagements were scoped, performance was measured against documented criteria, and two contractors were terminated following formal review, with structured recommendations submitted to UK leadership. Zero unresolved vendor disputes across the full engagement.

Communication between distributed leadership and ground operations was formalized through a defined protocol: WhatsApp for urgent matters only, email for everything else, no out-of-hours messaging except emergencies, all overtime communicated and documented in writing. Portuguese statutory holiday entitlements were tracked proactively and submitted to leadership each cycle — because off-site UK ownership does not monitor Portuguese labour law independently.

Results

The framework ran without structural changes for seven years. The systems I designed in the first year of the engagement are still the systems the organization runs on.

  • Zero coverage gaps across seven consecutive operational years
  • Zero unresolved vendor disputes across the full eight-year engagement
  • 50% workflow efficiency gain for off-site decision-makers against the informal baseline
  • 100% day-one operational readiness for every new hire and rotating contractor
  • UK principals have never needed to travel to Lisbon for routine governance

The sector context matters here. Healthcare and maintenance operations cannot absorb the kind of scheduling failures that a software team can work around. The zero-gap record is not an administrative metric. It is proof that the systems hold under real operational pressure.
