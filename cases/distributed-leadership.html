Communication and workload visibility system for distributed leadership

Operations · Systems design · Distributed-leadership coordination

Context

A private services company in Portugal, with three permanent staff and ownership permanently based in the UK. No on-site management. I am the sole HR function and have been for eight years, reporting directly to senior leadership in another country. The company also runs a network of seven external service providers across healthcare, maintenance, and specialized services.

Challenge

Without formal HR systems, the UK-based ownership had no real-time view into team operations. Workload distribution, overtime, vendor performance, and Portuguese statutory compliance, holiday entitlements specifically, were tracked informally through WhatsApp and memory. Leadership decisions about staffing and vendor retention were being made on incomplete information. Communication protocols between UK ownership and the Portuguese operation were undefined, which produced coordination friction and recurring miscommunication.

Approach

I built a series of operational systems to close the visibility gap. The core deliverable was an integrated tracking system covering team scheduling, external provider coordination, and budget monitoring. It became the single source of truth for off-site decision-makers. I also designed a three-document onboarding toolkit for incoming staff: shift schedules, role-specific operational profiles, and external contractor coordination documents. New team members reach full operational readiness on day one.

To formalize communication between distributed leadership and ground operations, I established a communication SLA: WhatsApp for urgent matters only, email for everything else, no out-of-hours messaging unless an emergency, and all overtime communicated and documented in writing. I built a proactive holiday entitlement tracking spreadsheet that I submit to leadership each cycle, because off-site UK ownership does not track Portuguese statutory holidays independently.

I created a vendor performance evaluation framework for the seven external service providers. Two contractors were terminated based on documented performance and conduct evaluation, with formal recommendations submitted through structured leadership meetings. I have maintained continuous workforce scheduling across a three-person team, balancing permanent and rotating staff against operational requirements over a seven-year period with zero coverage gaps.

Outcomes

  • 50% workflow efficiency gain for off-site decision-makers through real-time system visibility
  • Day-one operational readiness for all new hires through structured onboarding documentation
  • Zero coverage gaps over a seven-year operational period across a three-person team
  • Five of seven contractors retained based on documented performance criteria
  • Compliance maintained on Portuguese statutory holidays across all operational cycles

Skills demonstrated

Operational systems design, cross-functional coordination, distributed team management, vendor performance evaluation, statutory compliance, workforce scheduling, documentation, process mapping.
