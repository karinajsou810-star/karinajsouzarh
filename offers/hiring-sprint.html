The hiring sprint

Your open roles get filled. You stop living in your inbox.

2–3 weeks Fixed scope Fixed price One named outcome

What you get
  • Role scoping session — I map exactly who you need before sourcing begins, so we don’t spend two weeks on the wrong profiles
  • Active sourcing across the right channels for your role and seniority level
  • Screening at volume — 25 to 30 profiles evaluated per day against your actual criteria, not a generic checklist
  • A shortlist of qualified candidates with written evaluation notes, ready to send to your hiring managers
  • Interview scheduling coordinated end-to-end
  • Clean pipeline handover at the end of the sprint — structured, documented, yours to run forward
Who this is for

You have open roles and no recruiter. The hiring process is sitting in your inbox between product decisions and investor calls. You know what you need but you don’t have the hours to source, screen, and coordinate interviews at the pace the business requires. You need the roles filled in a defined window, and you need the work done without building out a function you’re not ready to staff.

How it works
  1. 1
    BriefDay one. Role scope, must-haves, deal-breakers, timeline. This is where most recruiters skip ahead — I don’t.
  2. 2
    SourcingActive outreach begins. I use the channel that fits the role: ATS databases, LinkedIn, referral networks, job boards. Not all four for every position.
  3. 3
    ScreeningProfiles evaluated daily against your criteria. Running shortlist maintained throughout. Strong candidates flagged as they surface, not at the end of the sprint.
  4. 4
    Shortlist and handoverA clean, documented shortlist with evaluation notes. Interview scheduling handled. Pipeline handed over in a format your team can use.
The numbers behind this
